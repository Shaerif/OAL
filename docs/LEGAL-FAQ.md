## üè¢ Attribution Requirements

### What happens if I forget to attribute?

Your license terminates automatically. You must:
1. Stop using the work immediately
2. Add proper attribution
3. Notify the licensor of your correction

If you correct within 30 days, the license may be reinstated.

### How prominent must attribution be?

Attribution must be:
- Visible to users
- Easy to find
- Clearly readable
- In an appropriate location for the medium

For software: Include in documentation, about pages, or source code.
For media: Include in captions, credits, or metadata.

### Can I put attribution in a separate file?

Yes, if:
- The file is included with the work
- Users can easily find it
- You reference the file location in visible places

Example: "See ATTRIBUTION.txt for license details"

### What if I modify the work?

You must:
- Keep original attribution
- Add your own copyright for changes
- Document what you changed
- Apply OAL to the original portions

## ü§ñ AI Content

### Does OAL cover AI-generated content?

Yes. OAL explicitly includes AI-generated code, media, and content. You must attribute both:
- The original work (if AI was trained on OAL-licensed material)
- The AI tool or system used

### Who owns AI-generated content under OAL?

Ownership depends on:
- Who initiated the AI generation
- What the original license says
- Local laws regarding AI-generated works

OAL requires clear identification of AI involvement.

### Can I use OAL-licensed work to train AI?

Yes, but:
- You must give attribution
- You must comply with all OAL terms
- You should document the training data source
- Consider data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA)

## üåç International Use

### Does OAL work in my country?

OAL is designed for international use. It complies with:
- Berne Convention
- TRIPS Agreement
- WIPO Copyright Treaty

However, local laws may affect enforcement. Consult a local attorney for specific jurisdictions.

### Which laws govern OAL?

The license follows the laws of the licensor's jurisdiction. For disputes:
- Courts in the licensor's jurisdiction have authority
- International arbitration is available
- Local laws apply for enforcement

### Can I enforce OAL across borders?

Yes. OAL includes provisions for:
- Cross-border enforcement
- International dispute resolution
- Recognition in multiple jurisdictions

Enforcement follows the procedures of the jurisdiction where the violation occurs.

## üîÑ Compatibility

### Can I combine OAL with other licenses?

Yes, with conditions:
- Keep OAL for OAL-licensed portions
- Comply with all applicable licenses
- Clearly separate different licensed content
- Document which parts use which licenses

### Is OAL compatible with GPL?

OAL and GPL have different requirements. Combining them requires careful separation:
- Keep OAL portions under OAL
- Keep GPL portions under GPL
- Do not mix code in ways that violate either license

### Can I change from OAL to another license?

The original work stays under OAL. You can:
- Add your own license for your modifications
- Dual-license your contributions
- Not remove OAL from the original work

- License termination
- Legal action
- Monetary damages

Always comply with license terms.

## üÜò Dispute Resolution

### What if someone violates my OAL-licensed work?

Steps to take:
1. Document the violation
2. Contact the violator informally
3. Request correction within 60 days
4. If unresolved, consider mediation
5. If still unresolved, consider legal action

### How are disputes resolved?

OAL provides a process:
1. Informal resolution (60 days)
2. Mediation
3. Arbitration (if agreed)
4. Court action

Parties should attempt informal resolution first.

### Can I sue for OAL violations?

Yes. OAL is legally enforceable. You can seek:
- Injunctions to stop violations
- Monetary damages
- Destruction of infringing materials
- Legal fees

Consult an attorney for specific cases.

## üéØ Special Cases

### What about fair use?

Fair use (or fair dealing) may apply in some jurisdictions. This is separate from OAL permissions. Fair use depends on:
- Purpose of use
- Nature of the work
- Amount used
- Effect on market

Consult a legal professional about fair use.

### Can I use OAL for government work?

Depends on jurisdiction. Some governments:
- Cannot use certain licenses
- Have special requirements
- Must use public domain
- Have specific procurement rules

Check local government regulations.

### What about educational use?

OAL permits educational use. You must still give attribution. Educational institutions should:
- Document all OAL-licensed materials
- Train staff on attribution requirements
- Include attribution in course materials
- Comply with institutional policies

### Can I use OAL for internal corporate projects?

Yes. OAL permits internal use within organizations. You must:
- Maintain attribution in internal documentation
- Keep license notices in source code
- Document OAL-licensed components in project inventories
- Ensure compliance if the project becomes public

### What happens if I accidentally violate OAL?

If you discover an accidental violation:
1. Stop using the work immediately
2. Add proper attribution
3. Notify the licensor of your correction
4. Document the correction process

If corrected within 30 days of discovery, the license may be reinstated.

### Can I sublicense OAL-licensed work?

Yes, you can include OAL-licensed work in larger projects and distribute them. However:
- You must keep OAL for the OAL-licensed portions
- You must provide attribution to the original licensor
- You cannot remove or modify the original license
- Recipients receive the same OAL rights

### What if the licensor cannot be contacted?

If you cannot contact the licensor:
- Still provide proper attribution
- Keep all license notices
- Document your attempts to contact
- Maintain records of your compliance

### Can I use OAL-licensed work in a proprietary product?

Yes, OAL permits commercial use in proprietary products. You must:
- Include attribution in user-facing materials
- Provide license information in documentation
- Keep OAL for the OAL-licensed portions
- Not claim ownership of the OAL-licensed work

### What about derivative works in different languages?

Translations and adaptations are derivative works. You must:
- Keep original attribution
- Add your own copyright for the translation
- State the nature of the adaptation
- Apply OAL to the original portions
- Provide attribution in the target language

### Can I use OAL-licensed work in training materials?

Yes. You must:
- Attribute the original work
- Include license information
- Not claim the work as your own
- Keep attribution in all distributed materials

### What if I want to change the license of my derivative work?

You can add your own license to your modifications, but:
- The original work remains under OAL
- You must keep OAL attribution for original portions
- Clearly separate your contributions
- Document which parts use which license

### Can I use OAL-licensed work in open source hardware?

Yes. OAL covers digital content including:
- Firmware source code
- Design documentation
- Schematics and diagrams
- Configuration files
- Associated software

You must provide attribution in hardware documentation.

### What about using OAL-licensed work in AI training?

You can use OAL-licensed work to train AI models if:
- You give attribution to the original work
- You comply with all OAL terms
- You document the training data source
- You consider data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA)

The AI-generated output may require attribution to both the original work and the AI tool used.
